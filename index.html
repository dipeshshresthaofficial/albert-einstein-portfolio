About Me

I (14 March 1879 – 18 April 1955) am a German-born theoretical physicist, widely acknowledged to be one of the greatest and most influential physicists of all time. I am best known for developing the theory of relativity, but also made important contributions to the development of the theory of quantum mechanics. Relativity and quantum mechanics are the two pillars of modern physics. My mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc2, which arises from relativity theory, has been dubbed "the world's most famous equation". My work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.

I moved to Switzerland in 1895, forsaking my German citizenship the following year. In 1897, at the age of 17, I enrolled in the mathematics and physics teaching diploma program at the Swiss Federal polytechnic school in Zürich, graduating in 1900. In 1901, I acquired Swiss citizenship, which I kept for the rest of my life.
